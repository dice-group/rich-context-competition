    "abstract": "Abstract\nThis paper investigates transfer pricing as tax avoidance before and after reforms of anti-\navoidance legislation. The reforms introduced and tightened obligatory documentation require-\nments for transfer prices to enforce that multinational enterprises (MNEs) set internal transfer\nprices at an arm's-length. Linking data from the Microdatabase Statistics on International\nTrade in Services that comprehends prices of MNEs' international service transactions to the\nMicrodatabase Direct Investment, I create a unique, novel data set to obtain information on\nwhether MNEs' transaction partners are affiliated companies or not. The results provide em-\npirical evidence for tax-motivated transfer pricing during the entire first decade of the 2000s.\nInterestingly, MNEs target different types of service transactions for profit shifting via trans-\nfer pricing depending on the at the time applicable anti-shifting legislation. The findings show\ntransfer pricing legislation to be effective in case of service transactions with observable market\nvalues. In contrast, the results clearly reveal the short-comings of transfer pricing legislation in\ncase of intellectual property (IP) where an effective enforcement of the arm's-length principle\nis very limited as market values are unobservable. Here, the findings suggest the need for a\nchange in tax policy in order to effectively prevent base erosion in case of IP-related transfer\npricing.\n",
