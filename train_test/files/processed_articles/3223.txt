    "abstract": "Abstract\nThis paper develops a theoretical model of dynamic decision-making of a monetary\npolicy committee with heterogeneous members. It investigates the optimal transparency,\nand the optimal way of transmitting information of committees, by analysing the effects\ndifferent communication strategies have on financial markets. It is shown that the\ncommunication strategy of the central bank committee has a significant effect on the\npredictability of monetary policy decisions when there is asymmetric information\nbetween the committee and market agents. Transparency about the diversity of views of\nthe committee surrounding the economic outlook makes future monetary policy more\npredictable. However, communicating the diversity of views regarding monetary policy\ndecisions may lead to less predictability of monetary policy in the short term. In addition,\nit is shown that communication in the form of voting records has the greatest effect on\nmarket participants' near term policy expectations. These results support findings of the\nempirical literature and have strong implications for the optimal communication\nstrategies of committees including the question whether individual voting records should\nbe published.\n",
