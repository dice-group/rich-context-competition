And 2) the hazard function of the time-since-last-adjustment is not constant.\nThis evidence brings difficulties for the widely used Calvo model that implies highly rigid\nprices at the micro level as well as a constant hazard function. In this paper, I construct a\ngeneralized time-dependent model of nominal rigidity and derive the generalized NKPC,\nconditional on a hazard function with an arbitrary shape.\nThe analytical solution shows that this version of the NKPC nests the Calvo NKPC in the\nsense that, given a constant hazard function, the effect of lagged inflation exactly cancels the\neffects of lagged expectation terms. In light of this result, we learn that lagged inflation and\nlagged expectations are not extrinsic to the time-dependent model. They are missing in the\nbasic Calvo setup, only because the restrictive 'memoryless' pricing assumption cancels them\nout. More importantly, inflation persistence generated by this optimizing model is not\nintrinsic. Instead, it results from the additional autoregressive terms of real marginal cost\nintroduced through lagged expectations. The inclusion of these new terms reflects the impact\nof past economic decisions on current outcomes. This paper formulates this idea in a\ntheoretical model.\nIn the numerical assessment, this paper makes two methodological contributions. Firstly,\nin the calibration exercise, I use the parsimonious Weibull duration model to parameterize the\nhazard function and the average price duration. Secondly, I log-linearize the NKPC around a\nnon-zero inflation steady state. Combining dynamic effects of the increasing hazard pricing\nand trend inflation is important because without trend inflation, the increasing-hazard model\nmerely changes the timing of pricing behavior. With trend inflation however, relative prices\ndisperse quickly with the elapsed time since the last adjustment and as a result, not only the\ntiming of the price adjustment but also the magnitude of the adjustment are affected by the\nincreasing hazard function. In addition, trend inflation affects all coefficients in the\ngeneralized NKPC. Thereby the change in the trend inflation affects the relative importance\nbetween the forward-looking and backward-looking terms in the Phillips curve.\nWhen simulating the full-scale general equilibrium model, simulation results show that\nthe model can jointly account for the following stylized facts from the data: 1) the higher the\ntrend inflation is, the more persistent the inflation gap becomes. 2) Trend inflation has a larger\neffect on the persistence of output and real marginal cost than on the persistence of inflation.\n3) The correlation between inflation and real marginal cost decreases further when trend\ninflation is high. 4) The impulse response of inflation to a nominal money growth shock is\nhump-shaped.\nNicht technische Zusammenfassung\nAktuelle Mikrodaten bieten einen \u00fcberzeugenden Beleg daf\u00fcr, dass beim Preisbildungs-\nprozess erstens die Preise auf der Unternehmensebene recht flexibel angepasst werden, ob-\nwohl die aggregierte Inflation sehr persistent ist und dass zweitens die Hazardfunktion f\u00fcr\nPreisanpassungen seit der letzten Preis\u00e4nderung nicht konstant ist.
