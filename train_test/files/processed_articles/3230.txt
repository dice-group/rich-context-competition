I examine this proposition in a small theoretical model emphasizing forward-\nlooking private sector behavior. Transparency makes it easier for price setters to infer the\ncentral bank's future policy intentions, thereby making current inflation more responsive to\npolicy actions. This induces the central bank to pay more attention to inflation rather than\noutput gap stabilization. Then, transparency may be disadvantageous.
