    "abstract": "Abstract\nWe consider the properties of two monetary policy rules (monetary targeting, Taylor-type interest\nrate rule) in an intertemporal equilibrium model with capital accumulation and two outside assets\n(government bonds, fiat money). The paper shows that the long-run behaviour of the economy\ndepends critically on whether under the monetary-fiscal regime the steady-state real interest rate\nis independent of inflation. If this is the case, there exists in our model a unique steady state with\nstable adjustment dynamics under either monetary policy rule. By contrast, if superneutrality fails,\ndynamics under the interest rate rule may suffer from global indeterminacy arising from multiple\nsteady states which do not necessarily differ in terms of the 'activeness' of the interest rate\nfeedback on inflation. This is ruled out under monetary targeting.\n",
