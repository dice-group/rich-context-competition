    "abstract": "Abstract:\nUnderstanding adjustment processes has become central in economics. Empirical analy-\nsis is fraught with the problem that the target is usually unobserved. This paper devel-\nops, simulates and applies GMM methods for estimating dynamic adjustment models in\na panel data context with partially unobserved targets and endogenous, time-varying\npersistence. In this setup, the standard first difference GMM procedure fails. I propose\nthree estimation strategies. One is based on quasi-differencing, and it leads to two dif-\nferent, but related sets of moment conditions. The second is characterised by a state-\ndependent filter, while the third is an adaptation of the GMM level estimator.\n",
